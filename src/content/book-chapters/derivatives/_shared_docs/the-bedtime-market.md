At bedtime, the market looks different. It is no longer a shouting arena full of charts and blinking fear. It becomes a quiet town where tomorrow's weather is still being guessed, negotiated, and gently priced.

This chapter uses story to make finance readable for children and still charming for adults. The point is not to oversimplify. The point is to make the first layer welcoming enough that curiosity can enter. Once the imagination relaxes, ideas like risk, timing, and exchange start to feel natural instead of hostile.

The Bedtime Market opens the manuscript with a promise: financial language can be taught without becoming dry, and abstraction can still carry warmth. A child does not need jargon first. A child needs shape, rhythm, and an image that can be remembered after the lights go out.

## What the story teaches

- Prices are conversations about tomorrow.
- Uncertainty can be explored without fear.
- Good explanation begins with image before it reaches formula.
