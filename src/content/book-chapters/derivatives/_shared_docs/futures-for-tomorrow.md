Children already understand futures, even if they do not use the word. "Tomorrow we trade snacks." "Next week I will save my sticker." "When summer comes, we will go." A future begins as a promise about later that shapes behavior now.

This chapter uses that intuition to explain contracts, expectation, and timing. Futures are not mystical. They are agreements that bring tomorrow into the present just enough for people to plan, commit, and coordinate. Once the story is framed this way, the machinery of finance starts to feel less alien.

The chapter also leaves room for wonder. Tomorrow is never fully known. That is why the promise matters. A future is a bridge built across uncertainty, and every bridge reveals what two people or two systems are willing to trust about the road ahead.

## Bridge logic

- A future lets tomorrow influence today's choices.
- Planning is a form of imagination with consequences.
- Markets become more understandable when promises are made visible.
