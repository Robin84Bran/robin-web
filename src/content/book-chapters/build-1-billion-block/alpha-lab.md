Every serious capital system needs a place where theory can meet weather. Alpha Lab is that place. It is where ideas lose their ceremonial polish and encounter slippage, variance, timing error, and the ordinary friction of reality.

The purpose of a lab is not prediction theater. It is controlled humility. A good lab gives the operator a repeatable way to test assumptions, isolate failure modes, and refine the rules before larger amounts of capital depend on them. It is how intuition graduates into process.

What makes a lab productive is selectivity. Not every hunch deserves full instrumentation. Not every backtest deserves emotional attachment. The system has to filter. It has to decide what is promising enough to keep, what belongs in archive, and what should never have left the notebook.

## Lab discipline

- Keep experiments small enough to learn from quickly.
- Record why a thesis worked, not just whether it worked.
- Let failed tests improve the system instead of becoming private embarrassment.
