Capital systems fail as often from state confusion as from bad information. A strong thesis still collapses if the operator cannot tell whether they are currently researching, executing, defending, recovering, or waiting.

Five States turns behavior into infrastructure. It argues that clear state transitions are one of the most under-valued forms of edge because they reduce emotional bleed across decisions. Research wants openness. Execution wants commitment. Recovery wants honesty. These are different modes, and mixing them is expensive.

The deeper point is practical: self-knowledge becomes part of portfolio construction. If a system cannot contain the operator's mood, ego, and urgency, it does not deserve larger scale. Psychological edge is not hype. It is the ability to remain readable to oneself when stakes rise.

## States worth naming

- Observe without forcing action.
- Commit when the structure is clear.
- Defend when the environment changes.
- Recover without losing the lessons.
- Wait long enough for the next signal to separate itself from noise.
